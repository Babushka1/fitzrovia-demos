✦ AI Design Brief — for the Investments & Development team
At Elm-Ledbury, the co-working lounge is the portfolio's highest-ROI square footage: $0.84 per use, touched by 61% of residents monthly, and associated with a +9.1pt renewal lift among users after controlling for tenure and rent delta. The golf simulator is the weakest on both usage and attributed premium — but the answer is not to trade prestige for commodity space. Luxury amenities carry option & signaling value: residents pay for what the building says about them, not only what they use. Recommendation: rotate the concept, keep the luxury. The same 1,100 sq ft footprint supports an F1 racing simulator, ski simulator, or golf-sim refresh on a 24–36 month rotation cycle — measured, then rotated again if reach stays <15%. This preserves the aspirational program while letting data pick which luxury earns its floor plate.

How to read this: Utilization = occupied-hours ÷ available-hours (fob + WiFi + sensor data). Monthly reach = % of residents using it ≥1×/month (unique fobs). Cost/use = (amortized capex + annual opex incl. metered energy, cleaning, staff share) ÷ annual uses. Attributed premium = hedonic regression coefficient: $/unit/month of achievable rent associated with this amenity across portfolio + market comps, controlling for unit size, floor, location, building age. Renewal lift = renewal rate of regular users vs matched non-users (matched on tenure, rent-to-market, unit type). Lift ≠ proof of causation — flagged accordingly. Prestige note: low usage ≠ low value — the attributed-premium column captures what residents pay for an amenity's existence (option & signaling value), independent of usage. "Rotate" verdicts apply only when an amenity underperforms on both usage and premium.
